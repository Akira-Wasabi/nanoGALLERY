data-ngdesc='This body of work was made by Audry for a collaborative exhibit with Carolyn Baginski. Images of the entire show can be seen in the "Sculpture Exhibits" folder. Below is the art statement from the exhibit:

Historic textile patterns from India share many elements that interest both of us. They combine our mutual love of floral patterning with Audry&#39;s interest in the historical evolution of floral prints and Carolyn&#39;s formal sensibilities regarding scale as well as her interest in narrative. These familiar Chintz patterns, upon closer look, are actually quite strange. Each textile is hand painted to depict the local floral and fauna. However, because the artists worked from memory, the translation from reality to image is full of oddities. The floral imagery has become far removed from natural plant life. For example, several different types of flowers can grow from one branch or slender twigs appear to hold an impossible amount of weight. The scale within each textile is wildly distorted. Everything exists on the same plane, yet a flower can be larger than a deer which can be larger than a mountain. This creates the illusion of worlds within worlds. These simulated environments pose the question of what our world would look like if these depictions were true.

We developed this body of work by examining and dissecting specific patterns, working separately to create individual elements and then coming together to install them. Much like the original artists, as we translated two dimensional images into three dimensional sculptures we made assumptions and interpretations about form and scale. Each object takes inspiration from its original source, and because of the nature of the process, becomes something new. In some places, plants appear to be growing across the walls, but in others, birds pose neatly as a figurine placed on a table. The resulting exhibition is a cross between a decorative space and a simulated natural environment.'
